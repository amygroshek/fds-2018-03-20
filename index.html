Gilbert & Gubar

Gilbert and Gubar argued that the "maddened doubles" in texts by women writers of the nineteenth and twentieth century "function as social surrogates," projecting women writers' anxiety of authorship in a male-dominated literary tradition (Donaldson, 99)

Feminism & Madness

  1. Contemporary to the development of the social model of disability
  2. Emphasis on authorship, makes sense for literary criticism... extension to disability studies...
  3. Presumed whiteness, cis- and straight-ness, and class position of the "mad" women in question

Donaldson

A feminist disability studies reading that stresses the connections between madness and physiognomy, between the mind and body, provides us with an alternate way of conceptualizing madness in Jane Eyre... to complicate the current constructions of madness within feminist theory. (102)

Impairment and Disability

A feminist disabilty studies reading... demands closer attention to physical bodies and to the theories of embodiment that structure the novel (110)

In particular, physiognomy in the 19th century... though Donaldson argues that it carries over into contemporary studies in neuroscience
