Productive Maiming

I argue that the Israeli state manifests an implicit "right to maim" and debilitate Palestinian bodies and environments as a form of biopolitical control.
I note that the policy of maiming is a productive one, through the profitability of what I call a speculative rehabilitative economy. (128)

Chapter Summary

This final chapter takes the biopolitics of debilitation to its furthest expanse, looking at how the population available for injury is capacitated for settler colonial occupation through its explicit debilitation. It moves the argumentation about debilitation from the production of populations available for injury to the targeting of populations to be injured. (128-29)

"Shoot to Cripple"

Shoot to cripple attempts to preemptively debilitate the resistant capacities of another intifada, the next intifada. (129)

"Humanitarian"

[U]nderstood in liberal terms as less violent than killing... shoot to cripple appears on the surface to be a humanitarian approach to warfare. (129)

Agamben

  • Taking off of Foucault
  • Ancient Greek concepts of bios and zoe
  • Bios == "life in society"
  • Zoe == "bare life/excluded life"
  • Sovereign power → decide who is bare life
  • Democracy → everyone holds sovereign power
  • Critical race studies → some human life is constructed as outside of bios from the start
Maiming is a practice that escapes definition within both legal and biopolitical or necropolitical frameworks because it does not proceed through making live, making die, letting live, or letting die (137)

Let Live, Make Die, or Will Not Let Die

If slow death is conceptualized as primarily through the vector of "let die" or "make die," maiming functions as "will not let die and, its supposed humanitarian complement, "will not make die." Maiming masqureades as "let live" when in fact it acts as "will not let die." (137)

Dehumanize by Withholding Death

Withholding death... becomes an act of dehumanization: the Palestinians are not even human enough for death.
Sanctioned maiming... bespeaks a profound failure in the global human rights framing of disability as a protected and supported social difference (141)
